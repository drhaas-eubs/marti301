Case Study

NovaBanc: To Automate or Augment?

Mid-sized European bank facing an AI strategy decision

NovaBanc is a mid-sized European bank with 4,200 employees, headquartered in Zurich. In Q1 2026, the CEO presented a board paper titled 'AI-First by 2028' proposing a €45 million investment in artificial intelligence across three divisions: retail banking, corporate lending, and compliance.

The bank's competitors have moved aggressively. UBS launched an AI-powered wealth advisory platform in 2025, reducing client onboarding time by 60%. A fintech challenger, Neon, is capturing younger customers with fully automated personal finance tools. NovaBanc's own digital transformation programme, launched in 2023, has delivered modest results: a chatbot handling 30% of routine enquiries and a fraud detection model with 78% accuracy.

The board is divided. The Chief Risk Officer warns that the EU AI Act's high-risk classification for credit scoring could expose the bank to regulatory fines if AI governance is inadequate. The Chief Technology Officer argues that the bank's data infrastructure — legacy systems from three previous mergers — is not ready for enterprise AI. The Head of Retail Banking sees AI as an existential necessity, citing Klarna's workforce reduction as evidence that banks that move slowly will lose market share to AI-native competitors.

The CEO has asked each board member to come to the next meeting with a strategic recommendation: Should NovaBanc pursue a cautious, compliance-first approach (governance before speed), an aggressive automation strategy (speed before perfection), or a targeted augmentation model (AI assists employees rather than replacing them)?

Your Task

You are an external strategy consultant presenting to the NovaBanc board. Using frameworks from the Unit 1 Slibrary, prepare a visual argument (maximum one page of sketched frameworks) that recommends a strategic direction. Your recommendation must address: (a) which AI maturity stage NovaBanc currently occupies, (b) where competitive advantage will come from, and (c) how governance constraints shape the timeline.
