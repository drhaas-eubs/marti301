Case Study

Meridian Capital: Bridging the AI Implementation Gap

Asset manager struggling with AI deployment

Meridian Capital Partners is a Zurich-based asset manager with €12.4 billion AUM, serving institutional clients. 85 investment professionals, 22-year top-quartile track record through fundamental equity investing.

In 2024, the board approved a €6 million 'AI Augmentation Programme': NLP for earnings call analysis, alternative data integration, and ML portfolio optimisation. Eighteen months later: the NLP tool works (40bps alpha contribution), alternative data is stalled in pilot (data quality issues, 8 months of vendor negotiations), and the portfolio optimiser was abandoned after backtesting revealed severe overfitting.

The CIO is frustrated: 'We spent €6 million and got one-third of what we promised. Are we structurally incapable of becoming AI-augmented, or are we just doing it wrong?' The Head of Technology responds: 'Our infrastructure was built for analysts reading PDFs, not ML pipelines. We need another €4 million.'

The board wants a clear-eyed assessment: What is the realistic path forward?

Your Task

You are Meridian's new Head of AI Strategy. Using Unit 6 Slibrary frameworks, prepare a board presentation diagnosing current state, explaining why two initiatives failed, and mapping a 24-month path forward. Include: (a) AI maturity assessment, (b) overfitting diagnosis via Backtesting Validation Loop, (c) Pilot-to-Scale Staircase roadmap, (d) revised cost estimate using TCO Iceberg from Unit 4.

Source: Scenario developed for MARTI301. Adapted from: Muldowney (2023) Plans & Trusts; López de Prado (2018); Markowitz (1952).
