Case Study

QuantEdge: The Hidden Costs of AI Ambition

Fintech credit scorer facing regulatory and cost pressure

QuantEdge is a London-based fintech founded in 2021 by two former Goldman Sachs quantitative analysts. The company offers AI-driven credit scoring for SMEs using alternative data. By early 2026, QuantEdge has processed €380 million in loan applications with a default rate of 3.1%. Series B: €28 million at €140 million valuation.

Three risks dominate the board discussion. First, the EU AI Act classifies credit scoring as high-risk AI, requiring conformity assessments. Compliance cost estimate: €4–6 million over 12–18 months. Second, an internal audit revealed the model performs significantly worse for businesses less than two years old (accuracy drops from 91% to 67%), creating potential algorithmic bias. Third, true AI platform costs — data acquisition (€2.1M/year), cloud compute (€1.4M/year), model retraining, compliance team (8 FTEs) — exceed projections by 3.2×.

The CEO must present a revised three-year financial plan. Core question: Can QuantEdge scale its AI advantage while managing regulatory risk, model bias, and escalating costs?

Your Task

You are QuantEdge's incoming Chief Strategy Officer. Using frameworks from Unit 4 Slibrary, build a one-page diagnostic addressing: (a) risk governance for the credit scoring model, (b) total cost analysis revealing hidden costs, (c) sustainability assessment of the AI infrastructure. Use at least one framework from each Slibrary (13–16).

Source: Scenario developed for MARTI301. Adapted from: EU AI Act (2024); Taleb (2007) The Black Swan; Strubell et al. (2019).
