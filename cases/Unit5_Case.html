Case Study

SynthVision: Who Owns What AI Creates?

Generative AI startup facing three IP crises

SynthVision is a Berlin-based AI startup that develops generative AI tools for architecture. Its flagship product, DesignForge, generates photorealistic 3D renderings from building specifications. Training data: 4.2 million architectural images (70% scraped from the web, 20% licensed stock, 10% synthetic).

Three IP issues surfaced simultaneously. First, a Danish architecture firm, Nordvik Associates, filed a cease-and-desist claiming DesignForge outputs bear 'substantially similar' aesthetic to their signature style. Second, Getty Images sent a licence compliance notice arguing 340,000 scraped training images are from their catalogue. Third, SynthVision's largest client, a Munich construction group, asks: 'Do we own the copyright in designs DesignForge generates for us?'

The CEO must present an IP strategy that resolves all three issues while preserving competitive position.

Your Task

You are SynthVision's external IP counsel. Using frameworks from Unit 5 Slibrary, analyse: (a) copyright status of outputs, (b) legality of training data under EU and US law, (c) contractual IP allocation for enterprise clients. Use at least one framework from each Slibrary (17–20). Identify the highest-risk issue.

Source: Scenario developed for MARTI301. Adapted from: Getty v. Stability AI (2023); US Copyright Office (2023); EU DSM Directive (2019).
