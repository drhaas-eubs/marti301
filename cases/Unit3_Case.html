Case Study

Maison Lumière: Luxury Meets the Algorithm

French luxury fashion house AI-CX tension

Maison Lumière is a French luxury fashion house founded in 1952, with 120 boutiques across Europe, Asia, and North America. Annual revenue is €2.8 billion, with a brand valued among the top 30 luxury brands globally. 35% of revenue comes from repeat clients who visit boutiques at least three times per year.

In 2025, the new Chief Digital Officer launched 'Project Aura' — a €25 million initiative to deploy AI across the customer journey: an AI styling assistant on the e-commerce platform, a conversational AI concierge for WhatsApp and WeChat in 12 languages, predictive analytics for clienteling, and sentiment analysis on social media.

Six months in, results are mixed. The AI styling assistant increased online average order value by 18%, but conversion rates dropped 5% — customers browse more but buy less. The WhatsApp concierge handles 60% of enquiries without human intervention, but three viral social media posts from dissatisfied customers — who felt the bot was 'robotic and undignified' — generated negative press. Boutique staff report that predictive clienteling alerts are helpful but arrive too late.

The CEO has asked the CDO: How do we deliver AI-enhanced experiences that feel worthy of a luxury brand? Where should AI be invisible, and where should the human touch remain dominant?

Your Task

You are an external CX consultant advising Maison Lumière. Using frameworks from the Unit 3 Slibrary, map the current customer journey, diagnose why each AI initiative is underperforming, and recommend adjustments. Use at least one framework from each Slibrary (9–12).
