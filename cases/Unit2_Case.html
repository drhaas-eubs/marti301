FreshCart: When the Algorithm Gets It Wrong

Online grocery ML demand prediction failure

FreshCart is a European online grocery delivery service operating in 14 cities across Germany, Austria, and Switzerland. In 2025, the company deployed a machine learning model to predict daily demand for perishable goods — fresh produce, dairy, and bakery items — with the goal of reducing food waste (previously 12% of inventory) while maintaining 98% product availability.

The model, trained on 18 months of historical order data, performed well in backtesting: mean absolute error of 4.2% and a 35% predicted reduction in waste. The data science team, consisting of three ML engineers, was confident. The Chief Operating Officer approved a full rollout across all 14 cities in January 2026.

Within six weeks, three problems emerged. First, the model systematically over-ordered organic products in smaller cities where demand patterns differed from the training data (drawn primarily from Munich and Vienna). Second, a cold snap in February caused a sudden spike in soup and comfort food orders that the model — trained on milder winters — completely missed. Third, customer complaints about unavailable items rose by 22%, because the model optimised for waste reduction at the expense of availability.

The COO is now facing pressure from the board. The CFO calculates that the model has cost €1.8 million in excess inventory and lost sales in Q1 alone. The Head of Customer Experience reports that NPS scores dropped 11 points. The data science team argues they need more data and time to retrain the model.

The CEO has called an emergency strategy meeting and wants answers: What went wrong? Should we roll back to manual forecasting? And if we keep the AI, what needs to change?

Your Task

You are FreshCart's newly hired Head of AI Strategy. Using frameworks from the Unit 2 Slibrary, diagnose what went wrong and propose a remediation plan. Your one-page visual argument must include at least one framework from each of the four Slibraries (5–8) and must address model quality, decision process, data infrastructure, and customer impact.
