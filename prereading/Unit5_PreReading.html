Can AI Be an Author? The Question Reshaping Copyright Law

A Guide to the Authorship Decision Tree
Key Framework: Authorship Decision Tree

Copyright law was designed for human creators. The fundamental assumption since the Statute of Anne (1710) has been that a human mind conceives an original work. AI challenges this at its root.

In 2023, the US Copyright Office denied copyright registration for AI-generated images in Zarya of the Dawn, stating the author 'did not have sufficient control over the generated output' (US Copyright Office, 2023). This introduced the Authorship Decision Tree — branching logic determining copyrightability based on human involvement.

The first branch: is there a human author? If a system generates output with no instruction beyond pressing a button, copyright does not attach. The second branch: did the human exercise sufficient creative control? Providing detailed prompts, selecting from outputs, editing results — these may constitute sufficient authorship.

The EU approaches differently. The DSM Directive (2019) created text and data mining exceptions (Articles 3 and 4) affecting AI training. Article 3 provides broad research exceptions. Article 4 permits commercial TDM unless rightsholders opt out.

For business leaders, the practical recommendation is to build human creative control into AI workflows by design. Document the creative choices — prompts, selections, edits. This documentation trail is your evidence of authorship.
