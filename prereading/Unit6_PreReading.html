Why Investment Firms Struggle with AI

Lessons from Muldowney on ML Adoption
Key Framework: Planning vs. Implementation Gap / Muldowney ML Adoption Model

The investment management industry presents a paradox. It is among the most data-intensive sectors, yet ML adoption remains uneven. As Muldowney (2023) observes, AI's adoption in equity investment management is still in its initial stages.

The core challenge is the planning-implementation gap. Strategic presentations promise AI-driven alpha. Implementation reality involves months of data cleaning, integration failures, and the discovery that backtested performance rarely survives live markets.

Muldowney identifies three stages. First: data — narrowing thousands of candidates to hundreds, then cleaning. This is unglamorous but essential. Second: ML captures non-linear patterns traditional statistics miss. But ML captures patterns, not causation, and the signal-to-noise ratio in financial data is exceptionally low. Third: NLP enables analysis of text to identify emerging themes.

The critical insight: human judgment remains essential. ML cannot replace understanding of market context, geopolitical risk, or management quality. The most effective model is augmentation — the Centaur Model from earlier units.

López de Prado (2018) warns specifically about overfitting. With enough parameters, any model can predict the past. The Backtesting Validation Loop with walk-forward methodology is the essential safeguard.

For asset managers: calibrate ambition to maturity. Start with data quality. Graduate to ML only when the foundation is solid.
