The AI Iceberg: What Your Business Case Is Not Telling You

Understanding the Total Cost of AI Ownership
Key Framework: Total Cost of AI Ownership (TCO Iceberg)

When organisations build the business case for an AI project, the costs in the proposal are typically compute infrastructure, software licences, and team salaries. These are the visible costs — the portion of the iceberg above the waterline. They are almost always a dramatic underestimate.

Research consistently shows that the true cost of deploying AI in production is three to five times the initial estimate (Iansiti and Lakhani, 2020). Hidden costs include: data acquisition and cleaning (60–80% of data science time), model retraining and monitoring (concept drift), governance and compliance, integration with legacy systems, and organisational change management.

The iceberg metaphor is structurally accurate. These costs are distributed across departments rather than concentrated in the AI budget. A compliance cost appears in Legal. Data cleaning appears in IT. Neither is attributed to the AI project.

For business leaders, the TCO Iceberg serves three purposes. First, diagnostic: challenge the team to quantify each hidden layer. Second, communication: an iceberg diagram is more persuasive than a spreadsheet. Third, planning: by making hidden costs visible upfront, organisations budget realistically.

The most dangerous sentence in an AI business case is 'and then we deploy.' Deployment is not an endpoint — it is the beginning of an ongoing operational commitment.
