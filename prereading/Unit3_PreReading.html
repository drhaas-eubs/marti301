Beyond NPS: Measuring What Matters in Customer Experience

Why One Metric Is Never Enough
Key Framework: NPS-CSAT-CES Triangle

In 2003, Frederick Reichheld published a Harvard Business Review article with an audacious title: 'The One Number You Need to Grow.' That number was the Net Promoter Score (NPS) — derived from one question: 'How likely are you to recommend this company to a friend?' The elegance of NPS was its simplicity (Reichheld, 2003).

Two decades later, NPS remains the most widely used CX metric. But its dominance has created a blind spot. NPS measures loyalty intention but not why they feel that way, how hard the experience was, or whether a specific transaction was satisfactory.

This is where the NPS-CSAT-CES Triangle becomes essential. Customer Satisfaction (CSAT) measures transaction-level quality. Customer Effort Score (CES) measures friction — research found that reducing customer effort is a stronger predictor of loyalty than increasing delight (Dixon, Toman and DeLisi, 2013).

Used together, the three metrics form a complete diagnostic. NPS tells you whether customers love you. CSAT tells you whether specific touchpoints are working. CES tells you whether you are making it easy. A company with high NPS but low CES has loyal fans fighting through a painful experience — a ticking time bomb.

AI transforms all three metrics. Sentiment analysis can generate continuous NPS proxies. Conversational AI interactions can be scored for CSAT in real time. CES can be computed automatically from clickstream data.

The strategic question is not 'which metric should we use?' but 'how do we balance all three, and where does AI shift the trade-off?'
